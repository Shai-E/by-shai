From Lessons to Lines of Code

🎓 The Teacher's Heart

For years, my "source code" was a lesson plan and my "users" were my students. Teaching taught me the most important lesson in development: Empathy. If a student doesn't understand a concept, it's rarely their fault — it's the delivery.

I realized that the digital world has the same problem. Too many apps are built for profit or data, and not enough are built for people. I decided to swap the whiteboard for a keyboard to build tools that empower, simplify, and delight.

🛠️ Building with Purpose

I don't just build "software." I build solutions for the people I love. This personal connection keeps my standards high and my UX human-centric.

🎮

Momma

I watched my mom get frustrated by constant ad-breaks in her favorite games. So, I built her a sanctuary. Momma is an ad-free hub for Sudoku, Wordle, 2048, and more — making sure her downtime is actually peaceful.

🎵

Shema

My songwriter friends had ideas scattered across voice memos and notebooks. I created Shema to be their digital studio — combining lyrics, recording, chords, and a teleprompter into one seamless portfolio.

💳

Isracard

Today, I bring that same user-first mindset to my role at Isracard. Working with a high-performing team of mobile developers, I help build and scale one of Israel's most essential financial tools.

🚀 The Mission

I believe that code is a superpower. While I spend my days refining enterprise-level features and my nights building niche tools for friends, my eyes are always on the horizon.

I am currently working on several secret projects with a singular goal: to eventually release an app that doesn't just solve a small problem, but changes the world for the better.
